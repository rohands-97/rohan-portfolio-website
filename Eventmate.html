Overview

The Problem

Busy working parents and couples want to celebrate special moments with their loved ones, but planning an event by themselves can be stressful and time-consuming.

At the same time, new event planners also struggle to find clients and promote their services. So the need exists on both sides.

Objective

The objective of this project was to help busy working parents and couples easily find reliable event planners who can arrange personal events for them. For this student project, we focused only on the customer-facing experience — an app that helps users discover, compare, and connect with event planners based on their needs.

My role

This was a student project during my IIM Skills UI/UX bootcamp. Since I had prior design experience, I took the lead designer role and worked with my team throughout the project. I helped guide the design thinking process, supported research, ideation & UI creation, and mentored the team during key design decisions.

Phase 1 : Research

User Interviews

To understand the problem better, we spoke to working parents and couples who had planned personal events like birthdays, weddings, anniversaries, and small parties. We conducted 8 phone interviews with people aged 25–45, living in urban and semi-urban areas.

Competitive Analysis

We explored existing wedding and party planner platforms to understand how users currently find, compare, and book event planners. For our research, we shortlisted 4 relevant competitors for deeper analysis around 2 criteria that we found in user interviews.

Key Gap

Trust is still a major gap in the market.

From the competitive analysis, we found that planner discovery is fairly clear in the current market, but trust is still a major issue. Most platforms show verified badges, but users may still wonder what “verified” actually means. Planner profiles usually show photos of their past events, but they often miss important details like event type, budget range, location and guest size in the events.

Response time is also not clearly shown on most planner profiles, which can make users unsure about when they will get a reply.

Our Solution

From our research, we learned that users were not comfortable making advance payments before connecting with the event manager, reason being trust. So, we focused on building trust before booking.

The app helps users find verified event managers based on their event type, location, and budget. Users can view profiles, check past work, read reviews, and understand pricing before making a decision.

After that, users can send an event request with their event details. The event manager can review the details and accept the request. Once the request is accepted, both sides can connect through chat.

Conclusion

Next Step

Most of the decisions made for this platform have been based on the insights we gathered through our user interviews & competitive analysis in the research stage of this project. However, an appropriate next step would be to test our assumptions and validate the user experience choices that we have made. After that, we would need to build out other crucial features for the platform like booking agreement / payment flow & profile screen.

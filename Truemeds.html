Purchase Flow Revamp

Introduced a membership program for Truemeds to reduce cart dropoffs and improve customer retention.

By : Rohan Das

Context

About Truemeds

Truemeds is an Indian online pharmacy platform that helps users save upto 51% on medicine bills through discounts and affordable alternatives to branded medicines. Users can upload prescriptions, compare medicine options, and get medicines delivered to their doorstep.

Who are the users ?

Truemeds users are mainly people who regularly buy medicines online for themselves or their family members and are highly conscious about savings, trust, and convenience. Many of them are repeat medicine buyers, such as patients with chronic conditions, caregivers ordering for parents, and budget-conscious users who compare prices before placing an order.

The Problem Discovery

While purchasing medicine on Truemeds, I noticed that the final cart price was much higher than what I previously checked on product page. This change in price was mainly due to delivery charges.

The Problem

As most of the users of truemeds are repeat medicine buyers and conscious about savings, this delivery charges can create hesitation at the final step because the feeling of savings reduces just before placing the order.

Competitive Analysis

To understand how other online pharmacy platforms handle this issue. I analyzed 4 major competitors: Apollo 24/7, Netmeds, Tata 1mg, and PharmEasy.

Key Findings

Membership is an Expected Feature.

Most major competitors offer membership plans, making it a common feature in online pharmacy apps.

Free delivery drives value

Free delivery is the most common benefit across all competitor platforms.

Affordable plans reduce friction

Competitor have priced their membership low which makes it easier for their user to try the plan.

Cart is the right promotion point

Competitors promote membership near checkout, where users immediately notice delivery charges and savings.

Key Gap

Truemeds lacks a membership program unlike its competitors.

Since the primary goal of the platform is to help user save extra on medicine bills, a membership program can make savings feel more predictable through benefits like free delivery, cashback etc. This can further reduce cart hesitation due to delivery charges and encourage repeat purchases.

Benefit Prioritization

I prioritized membership benefits based on three key factors on user : Savings impact, Purchase confidence & Repeat order potential. The final benefits were selected by comparing user needs with common patterns found in leading online pharmacy membership plans.

The Challenge

How to introduce the membership plan to our users so that the users feel they are saving more and get encouraged to opt the membership plan?

Solution

Instead of creating only a separate membership page, I decided to introduce membership across the purchase flow. This helps users notice the benefit exactly when they are thinking about price, delivery charges, and final savings. The focus was not just to introduce the plan, but to help users understand why the plan is useful at the right moment.

User Testing

To validate the redesigned purchase flow, I tested it with 4 participants who regularly order medicines online. The goal was to understand whether the membership savings were clear to users and whether they would choose the membership plan.

1 Key task
8 Participants
30 mins Duration

The Task

Buy this medicine from Truemeds and check if there is any way to save more before placing the order?

Key Findings

Membership was explored from cart page.

Most participants clicked on the membership card from the cart screen to check its benefits and pricing.

Bottom sheet did not prioritize price.

Participants saw the benefits first, but the price and savings comparison were not clear enough to scan quickly.

Product page membership card was distracting.

During the task, the product page card pulled attention away from the main action i.e medicine price and add-to-cart.

Participants mentioned it’s value for money.

After seeing the membership price and benefits, participants understood the long-term savings and showed interest in buying the plan.

UI Iterations

Based on the insights from user testing, I made iterations to the membership card on the product page and the benefit bottom sheet on the cart page.

The Impact

In usability testing, all participants understood the value of the membership and showed intent to purchase the subscription. This indicates strong potential to reduce cart drop-off and improve customer retention, which can be further validated after product launch.

Takeways

This was my first solo project as part of my IIM Skills bootcamp, and it helped me understand how product design decisions can connect user needs with business goals.

• I learned how to test my design assumptions with users and convert feedback into clear iterations.

• I also learned how small moments in the purchase flow, like delivery charges and savings nudges, can strongly influence user confidence.

• This project helped me improve my UI decision-making and gave me hands-on experience working with design systems.
